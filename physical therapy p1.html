physical therapy after injury

Physical therapy may include exercises, manual therapy techniques, stretching, and modalities like heat or ice therapy. The goals of physical therapy are to reduce pain, increase range of motion, restore muscle strength, improve balance and coordination, and facilitate the return to normal activities or sports.

  • Rehabilitation Focus: Physical therapy is a rehabilitative treatment approach aimed at improving mobility, strength, and function after an injury.


  • Customized Treatment Plan: A physical therapist develops an individualized treatment plan based on the specific injury, taking into account the patient's needs and goals.


  • Exercise Programs: Physical therapy typically involves exercises that target specific areas affected by the injury, helping to restore flexibility, range of motion, and strength.


  • Manual Therapy Techniques: Physical therapists may use hands-on techniques such as massage, joint mobilization, or manual stretching to promote healing and improve movement.


  • Modalities: Therapeutic modalities like heat or ice therapy, ultrasound, electrical stimulation, or laser therapy may be employed to alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and promote tissue healing.


  • Pain Management: Physical therapy can include pain management strategies to address discomfort during the recovery process, ensuring the patient's comfort and overall well-being.
